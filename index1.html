Module Purpose

Purpose

This module establishes correct understanding, respect, and orientation around ilekes as spiritual instruments — not adornment. Students learn what ilekes are, who they belong to, how they are worn, and when they are authorized.

Key Principle: An ileke is worn because of authority — not preference.

Primary Ileke Identification

Common Ileke Examples (General Reference)

Note: Patterns and bead orders vary by lineage. This table is a foundation reference for identification and discussion.

Orisha Typical Colors Core Function Notes
Elegba (Èṣù / Elegguá) Red & Black (sometimes White) Roads, openings, communication Often first ileke received. Lineage-specific patterns.
Obatala Pure White (sometimes clear crystal) Consciousness, ethics, clarity Typically no mixed colors.
Oshun (Ọ̀ṣun) Yellow / Gold / Amber Love, fertility, abundance Often honey-toned glass or amber beads.
Yemọja (Yemaya) Blue & White Motherhood, protection, oceans Some lineages use multiple shades of blue.
Shango (Ṣàngó) Red & White Justice, leadership, thunder Often evenly alternating patterns.
Ogun (Ògún) Green & Black Iron, labor, strength May include metal accents depending on house.
Oya (Ọya) Purple / Burgundy / Brown Change, winds, death/rebirth Highly lineage-specific beadwork.
Orunmila (Ifá) Green & Yellow Destiny, wisdom, divination Typically reserved for Ifá priests (authorization required).
Authorization Rules

Authorization Rules

  • Ilekes are received, not self-assigned.
  • Bead counts, order, and materials are lineage-specific.
  • Unauthorized wearing is spiritually improper and can cause confusion.
  • Iyawos follow stage-based permissions and coverings.
Iyawo Guidelines

Iyawo Guidelines (General)

  • Wear only the ilekes you have been authorized to wear.
  • Some houses require covering ilekes in public — follow your instruction.
  • Remove ilekes for sleep, bathing, and intimacy unless told otherwise.
  • Do not mix Orisha ilekes without your elder’s direction.
Care & Handling

Care & Handling

  • Store ilekes off the floor, in a clean, respectful place.
  • Do not allow casual handling by others.
  • Clean only if permitted and never with harsh chemicals.
  • Do not place ilekes on shrines/altars without instruction.
